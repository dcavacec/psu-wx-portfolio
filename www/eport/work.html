Skills/Experience Attained Customer Service, Troubleshooting, Problem Solving, Resourcefulness

I began working for Penn State ITS as a rover in October of 2007. The position required that I maintain the printers in several labs across campus over a two hour shift, and address any problems or concerns users had as I walked between locations. A thorough understanding of the software applications on both Windows and Macintosh platforms was also required. The rover was primarily concerned with clearing paper jams, solving hardware issues with the printers or computers, and keeping inventory. As a rover, I was responsible for helping users with questions, and most labs also had a stationary consultant for that purpose.

In 2008, the rover and consultant divisions of computer lab services combined into one group. This meant that all ITS lab employees were now responsible for the same tasks and duties, while roving and consulting shifts would simply determine whether you were stationary or moving during the two hours. Personally, the merge meant getting a lot of additional customer service experience, as every shift now included interacting with users with issues ranging from printing to wireless connectivity. Also, the development of the ITS wiki page helped develop a resourceful and collaborative mindset at work.

College Works Painting, Inc.
Position Branch Operator
Duration February 2009 - July 2009
Core Responsibilities Marketing and Sales
Recruiting, Hiring, and Managing
Customer Service
Links National Services Group
Skills/Experience Attained Database Management, Customer Service, Planning

I joined the College Works painting team in February of 2009, to begin working as a branch operator in the State College, PA region. After several days of training and preparation, I began forming a business plan to build a database of leads and clients for residential and business exterior painting jobs. This involved reviewing historical patterns of jobs in the area, contacting local news agencies, and locating sections of ideal candidates in the neighborhoods surrounding Penn State University. After preferred areas had been targeted, leads were collected by cold and warm calling, advertising, and referrals.

Eventually, a team of marketers was recruited to help continue the growth of my database while I began estimating jobs for my potential clients. This was a valuable experience through which I learned the importance of expectations and setting goals in a business. By the end of my campaign, the team had more than doubled my leads.

Estimating exterior paint jobs required quick and accurate analysis of the potential client's needs while documenting the process thoroughly in order to satisfy any questions from the customer. Many factors had to be considered in completing the estimate, including preparation, required time, maintenance, paint type options, materials budget, and many others. The position required constant scheduling and dynamic planning of building the leads and clients database, performing estimates and sales, and maintaining a relationship with my clients.
