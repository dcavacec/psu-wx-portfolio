Welcome to the home of the Cane Coders, the leading edge in hurricane track and intensity prediction. This project took place during the spring semester of 2008, and involved gathering, comparing and analyzing hurricane data in the Atlantic Basin in order to develop a statistical tool used to model future hurricane motion and intensity. 

Information available on our website:

Societal impact of project:

Hurricanes and tropical storms have destructive capibilities for many of Earth’s inhabitants.  It is estimated that over the course of the past two centuries about 1.9 million people have perished due to the effects of these storms.  Some of these effects include heavy winds, flooding, storm surge, tornadoes, and debri damage.  Many improvements can be made in forecasting hurricane tracks and intensity in the coming years.  With hope of improving this forecasting ability, we aim to increase preparation time in order to minimize the damaging effects on society.  By utilizing a combination of synoptic and storm variables, better statistical models can be created.  As observation techniques improve along side with improvements made on statistical models, hurricane track intensity will be improved.
